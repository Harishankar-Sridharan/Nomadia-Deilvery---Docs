# Case-Studies

Subsectorization automatically splits a primary zone into individual subzones based on its associated postal codes. Use this feature to instantly create precise territories without needing mission data or manual balancing. It is the fastest path to a fully structured delivery area.

### Getting Started

Prerequisites and initial setup:
* An existing primary zone with multiple associated postal codes.
* Access to the **Configuration** module.

1. Head to the **Configuration** module and open **Manage Studies and Zones**.

![Frame at 1:49](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_1_to_49.png "Manage Studies and Zones – The menu location for accessing study configurations.")

2. Find the study you are working with and click the **Edit** button.

![Frame at 1:54](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_1_to_54.png "Edit button – The action to modify study details.")

### Feature Overview

* **Zone tab**: The area within a study where you manage primary zones and subzones.

![Frame at 1:54](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_1_to_54.png "Zone tab – The navigation tab for viewing geographic zones.")

* **Actions menu**: This menu contains tools to modify your selected primary zone.

![Frame at 2:23](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_23.png "Actions menu – The dropdown containing the subsectorization tool.")

* **Subsectorize based on zip codes**: This tool instantly generates one subzone for every postal code in your zone.

![Frame at 2:27](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_27.png "Subsectorize based on zip codes – The specific command to trigger automatic splitting.")

* **Zone table**: This list displays the generated subzones and their properties immediately.

![Frame at 2:39](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_39.png "Zone table – The list view showing created subzones.")

### How To: Subsectorize a Primary Zone

1. Navigate to the **Zone tab** inside your study.

![Frame at 1:54](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_1_to_54.png "Zone tab – Accessing the primary zone list.")

2. Select the **primary zone** you want to subsectorize.

![Frame at 2:01](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_01.png "Primary Zone – Selecting the specific zone containing postal codes.")

3. Open the **Actions menu**.

![Frame at 2:23](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_23.png "Actions menu – Opening the list of zone operations.")

4. Click **Subsectorize based on zip codes**.

![Flow 2:27 to 2:44](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_27_to_2_to_44.gif "Subsectorization flow – Selecting the option and viewing the instant generation of subzones.")

5. Review the new subzones listed in the **Zone table**.

![Frame at 2:39](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_39.png "Zone table – Verifying the created subzones.")

6. Verify the geographic boundaries on the **map**.

![Frame at 2:44](../images/Case-Studies-sub_sector_using_postal_code_timestamp_2_to_44.png "Map view – Visualizing the new subzone boundaries inherited from postal codes.")

### Productivity Tips

* 💡 **Instant Setup**: Create dozens of subzones in one click without loading mission data or waiting for processing.
* 💡 **Maximum Precision**: Use this method when postal code boundaries are the most accurate territory markers in your market.
* ⚠️ **Workload Balancing**: Avoid this feature if you must balance workloads across missions; use the Territory Manager instead.
